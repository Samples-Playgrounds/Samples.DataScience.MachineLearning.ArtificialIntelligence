Automated questionnaires are used in many fields—from market research and healthcare to education and customer feedback. The types of questions you include depend on your goals, but here are the most common categories and examples:

---

### **1. Closed-Ended Questions**
**Purpose:** Easy to analyze, good for quantitative data.
**Types:**
- **Multiple Choice:** Select one or more options.
  *Example:* "Which of the following social media platforms do you use? (Select all that apply) [Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, Other]"
- **Likert Scale:** Measure agreement or frequency.
  *Example:* "How satisfied are you with our service? [1 (Very Dissatisfied) to 5 (Very Satisfied)]"
- **Yes/No:** Simple binary response.
  *Example:* "Have you used our product in the last month? [Yes/No]"
- **Ranking:** Order preferences.
  *Example:* "Rank these features in order of importance to you: [Price, Quality, Customer Support]"

---

### **2. Open-Ended Questions**
**Purpose:** Collect qualitative data, deeper insights.
**Types:**
- **Short Answer:** Brief text response.
  *Example:* "What do you like most about our website?"
- **Long Answer/Essay:** Detailed feedback.
  *Example:* "Describe your experience with our customer service team."

---

### **3. Demographic Questions**
**Purpose:** Segment responses by group.
**Examples:**
- "What is your age group? [Under 18, 18-24, 25-34, etc.]"
- "What is your highest level of education? [High School, Bachelor’s, Master’s, etc.]"
- "What is your occupation?"

---

### **4. Behavioral Questions**
**Purpose:** Understand actions and habits.
**Examples:**
- "How often do you shop online? [Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Rarely]"
- "What factors influence your purchase decisions?"

---

### **5. Psychographic Questions**
**Purpose:** Explore attitudes, values, and lifestyles.
**Examples:**
- "How important is sustainability to you when choosing a product?"
- "What are your main sources of news?"

---

### **6. Screening Questions**
**Purpose:** Filter respondents.
**Examples:**
- "Are you currently employed? [Yes/No]"
- "Do you own a smartphone? [Yes/No]"

---

### **7. Matrix Questions**
**Purpose:** Efficiently collect data on multiple related items.
**Example:**
| Feature       | Very Important | Somewhat Important | Not Important |
|---------------|-----------------|--------------------|---------------|
| Price         | O               | O                  | O             |
| Quality       | O               | O                  | O             |

---

### **8. Contingency Questions**
**Purpose:** Follow up based on previous answers.
**Example:**
- "If you answered ‘No’ to the previous question, why not?"

---

### **9. Rating Scales**
**Purpose:** Measure intensity or quality.
**Examples:**
- "On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to recommend us to a friend?"
- "Rate your experience with our app: [★★★★★]"

---

### **10. Image/Video-Based Questions**
**Purpose:** Gather feedback on visual content.
**Example:**
- "Which logo do you prefer? [Image A / Image B]"

---

### **Tips for Effective Questionnaires**
- **Keep it short:** Respect respondents’ time.
- **Mix question types:** Balance closed and open-ended.
- **Avoid leading questions:** Don’t bias responses.
- **Pilot test:** Check for clarity and flow.
